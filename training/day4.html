Day 4: Presentation & Print Visit

Objective: To apply your accumulated knowledge in a practical presentation and witness the printing process firsthand. This day bridges theory and practice, preparing you for client-facing and operational roles.

Morning Session: Reflection & Knowledge Briefing

The culmination of your training this week will be a group reflection and briefing session. This exercise helps consolidate your learnings and ensures everyone clearly understands key concepts before moving forward.

  • Summary Preparation: You will work individually or in small groups to summarize the core concepts covered in the past three days, including printing types, vendor systems, cost estimation, and retail terminology.
  • Knowledge Briefing: Each group will prepare a short presentation or verbal briefing outlining their understanding, important takeaways, and any areas where they still have questions.
  • Presentation Skills: You will receive tips on how to clearly and confidently share your learnings with the team, focusing on clarity, confidence, and practical articulation.

Afternoon Session: Print Unit Visit

Time to see where the magic happens! We will visit one of our key print partners to see the production process up close. Please wear closed-toe shoes for safety.

  • Machine Demonstrations: See large-format printers, vinyl cutters, and laminators in action. Understand their capabilities and limitations.
  • Quality Control in Practice: Observe how our vendor partners implement QC at every stage, from pre-press to finishing and packing.
  • Meet the Experts: You'll have the opportunity to speak with the print operators and managers, ask questions, and build relationships.

Evening Debrief & Wrap-up

We'll conclude the training back at the office with a group discussion and reflection session.

  • Key Learnings: Share your biggest takeaways from the week and the print visit.
  • Surprises & Insights: What surprised you the most? What new insights did you gain into our processes?
  • Feedback & Next Steps: A chance to provide feedback on the training program and discuss your next steps as you transition into your role.
